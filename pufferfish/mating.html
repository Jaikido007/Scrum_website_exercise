Matting information


Spawning occurs after males slowly push females to the water surface or join females already present

The eggs are spherical and buoyant.

Hatching occurs after roughly four days.

The fry are tiny, but under magnification have a shape usually reminiscent of a pufferfish.

They have a functional mouth and eyes, and must eat within a few days.
